Machine learning (ML) is an area of artificial intelligence that makes use statistical tools and techniques to give computer software and programs the ability continuously improve in the performance or execution of a specific task (learn) from data, without any form of explicitly automatic programming. Machine learning can be applied in the following ways as seen below: 1. Medical Diagnosis: Machine Learning makes use of algorithms to process a large amount of data and information as well as detect more patterns than their human beings can. It is used mostly in medical diagnostics as in identifying breast cancer early in women. 2. Financial Trading: Trading firms (like Goldman Sachs) make use of machine learning algorithm to predict movements in the financial stock market such as increase or decrease in stock value. 3. Games: Machine Learning algorithms and techniques are also employed in the making of complex and intelligent games like Chess, Checkers, Tic-Tac-Toe. 4. Recommendations: Machine learning algorithms are adopted by companies like Amazon and Netflix to analyze their customer activities on their platform, and with that predict and recommend products and services they might be interested in. 5. Fraud Detection: Machine learning is also used by the government in spotting potential cases of fraud across many different fields (Financial, Health). Online payment integration system makes use of Machine Learning to fight money laundering.

Data Visualization is the graphical and pictorial representation of data in order to give insights into the trends and patterns contained within the data. Data Visualizations help in exploratory data analysis because it gives a much-enhanced view of a data frame such that certain things (outliers etc.) that would have gone undetected without the use of data visualizations become detectable (seen). In R programming language, there are several data visualization tools (i.e. tools that aid data visualization). The most commonly used ones are: Bar chart, Histogram, Heat maps, Scatter Plots, Box plots, Correlograms, Area charts. 1. Bar Chart — A Bar Chart is a graphical representation of data in which the data is represented using Rectangles. The value of the variable determines the length of the rectangles. There are different types of bar charts vis-à-vis Simple Bar Chart, Stacked Bar Chart and Clustered Bar Chart. 2. Histogram — Histogram is similar to bar chat but the difference is it groups the values into continuous ranges (while a bar chart has space(s) between each variable rectangle, a histogram doesn’t have space but is represented in a continuous fashion). Each bar in histogram represents the height of the number of values present in that range. 3. Heat maps — A heat map is a graphical representation of data where the individual values contained in a matrix are represented as colors. 4. Scatter Plots — Scatterplots show many points plotted in the Cartesian plane. Each point represents the values of two variables. One variable is chosen on the horizontal axis and another in the vertical axis. 5. Box Plots — Boxplots are a measure of how well distributed is the data in a data set. It divides the data set into three quartiles. This graph represents the minimum, maximum, median, first quartile and the third quartile in the data set. It is also useful in comparing the distribution of data across data sets by drawing boxplots for each of them. Boxplots are used for both continuous and categorical data. The categorical data is plotted on one axis i.e. x-axis while the continuous data is plotted on the other axis i.e. y-axis. 6. Correlograms — A correlogram also known as an autocorrelation plot, is an image of correlation statistics. It is a visual way to show a serial correlation in data that changes over time (i.e. time series data). Serial correlation (also called autocorrelation) is where an error at one point in time travels to a subsequent point in time. For example, in time series analysis, a correlogram is a plot of the sample autocorrelations versus (the time lags). 7. Area Chart — An area chart otherwise known as area graph graphically displays quantitative data. It is based on the line chart. The area between axis and line are commonly emphasized with colors, textures and hatchings. Commonly one compares two or more quantities with an area chart.

The aim of the study is to develop an automatic business analytics application that will describe, predict and prescribe solutions/suggestions on sectors of a business in order to enable quality decision making. Essentially, this paper is aimed at providing a practical approach (business analytics) to the knowledge base of enabling decision makers (including management accountants) in decision making. The objectives of the study include to: 1. Show the relationship between business analysis and decision making. 2. Establish the importance of management decision making. 3. Determine key performance indicators for the sectors of the business being analyzed. 4. Carry out a study of existing business analytics tools. 5. Design and model an automatic business analytics tool to support management decision making. 6. Implement the automatic business analytics tool to enable management decision making.
